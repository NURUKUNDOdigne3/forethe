Service 01
Nettoiement

Forest Cleaning

Selective removal of unwanted vegetation — shrubs, competing hardwoods, and invasive species — that threatens the health and growth of desirable tree species. This treatment creates the ideal conditions for a thriving, productive forest stand.

  • Selective cutting of competing vegetation
  • Removal of diseased or damaged stems
  • Promotion of target species regeneration
  • Manual and mechanized methods available
  • Applicable to both public and private forests

Service 02
Dégagement mécanique

Mechanical Clearing

Mechanized plantation clearing ensures young trees have unobstructed access to sunlight, water, and nutrients. Using specialized equipment, we efficiently remove competing vegetation around planted seedlings to maximize their survival and growth rate.

  • Brushcutter and mulcher operations
  • Protection of planted seedlings
  • Access to light and nutrients for young trees
  • Large-scale area treatment capability
  • Hydro-Québec right-of-way clearing

Service 03
Éclaircie précommerciale

Pre-commercial Thinning

Reducing stand density before trees reach commercial size is one of the most impactful silvicultural investments. By removing excess stems, we redirect the forest's energy into fewer, higher-quality trees — improving diameter growth, wood quality, and long-term timber value.

  • Stand density reduction to optimal spacing
  • Improved diameter and height growth
  • Enhanced wood quality and timber value
  • Reduced risk of windthrow and disease
  • Prescription-based intervention plans

Forest Plantation

Reforestation is the foundation of a sustainable forestry future. Our crews plant thousands of seedlings each season with precision and care — ensuring proper spacing, species selection, and soil preparation to give each tree the best possible start.

  • Species-appropriate seedling selection
  • Proper planting depth and spacing
  • Site preparation and soil assessment
  • Post-planting survival monitoring
  • Public, private, and Hydro-Québec mandates
